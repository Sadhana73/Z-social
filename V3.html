01 — Platform coverage

Analytics across the widest network range

Before comparing metrics, the first question is simple: does the tool even report on the networks you use? Zoho Social covers 11 platforms — more than any competitor here. The two biggest gaps elsewhere are Google Business Profile (unsupported by Hootsuite, Sprout Social, and Buffer) and Mastodon (exclusive to Zoho Social). Bluesky is supported by Zoho Social and Sprout Social only. On the core four — X, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn — all tools are level.

Buffer has the narrowest scope: TikTok, YouTube, Threads, Pinterest, Mastodon, and Bluesky are all absent from its analytics. Its Analyze dashboard is also paid-only — free users see only a basic "Sent" tab. All four tools explicitly exclude WhatsApp, Telegram, Instagram Personal, Facebook Groups, and LinkedIn Personal profiles from analytics.

03 — Post sorting, filtering & comparison

Find your best content — and understand why it worked

Knowing post-level metrics is only half the picture. The other half is being able to sort, filter, and rank your posts to surface what's actually performing — and why. All four tools let you sort posts and identify top performers, but the depth of filtering and comparison varies significantly. Sprout Social's ability to sort by custom metrics requires Premium Analytics. Buffer's hashtag table is limited to your top 5 per date range. 04 — Dashboard & interface

Analytics built around how you actually work

Having the right data is only useful if the interface makes it accessible. All four tools provide a unified cross-channel home dashboard and per-network drill-downs, but the flexibility of those dashboards differs substantially. The most significant gap is the drag-and-drop dashboard builder: Zoho Social is the only tool that lets you visually rearrange and customise your analytics layout without an add-on. On mobile, Zoho Social and Sprout Social offer full analytics access in their mobile apps. Buffer has no mobile analytics — the Analyze dashboard is web-only. Hootsuite's mobile app is available but analytics depth on mobile is not fully documented.

05 — Data freshness & historical depth

How current is the data — and how far back can you look?

Freshness and historical depth are two different things, and tools trade off between them. Zoho Social updates analytics in real time across networks. Sprout Social applies platform-dependent delays — Facebook demographics lag 48–72 hours. Hootsuite refreshes most reports every few minutes but Facebook reports update only once daily. Buffer applies up to a 24-hour delay across most networks. LinkedIn data refreshes after 24 hours in all four tools — that's a LinkedIn API constraint, not a platform choice.

On historical depth, Hootsuite goes furthest — data is available from January 1, 2018 across networks, with Instagram audience and Stories backfill on connect. Buffer provides meaningful backfill at the point of connection: 6 months of Facebook and LinkedIn channel-level data, 30 days of Instagram posts. Sprout Social supports up to 2 years of Instagram data, but other networks start from connection date. Zoho Social's historical data largely starts from the day you connect — no pre-connection backfill for post-level data, which means year-over-year comparisons aren't available until you've used it for a year or more.

06 — Audience demographics

Know exactly who you're reaching

Demographics tell you whether you're reaching the right people — not just how many. Zoho Social covers demographics across five platforms — Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, and Pinterest — and its LinkedIn breakdown is the deepest in this comparison: industry, job function, seniority, company size, experience level, and location. This makes it the strongest option for B2B teams who need to verify they're reaching decision-makers, not just accumulating followers.

Hootsuite covers the most platforms for demographics (six, adding TikTok and X) but offers less depth per platform. Sprout Social covers Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn. Buffer covers Instagram fully and Facebook for location only — age and gender aren't available even on paid plans there.

Meta deprecated Facebook age and gender audience data on November 15, 2025. All four tools now show follower count and location only for Facebook — this is a Meta API change that affects every tool equally. Marketing copy for any of these tools should focus on LinkedIn, Instagram, and YouTube for demographic claims.

07 — Organic vs paid analytics

Separate what you earned from what you spent

Organic vs paid analytics matter most on Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn — the networks where boosting and sponsored content are part of the strategy. On X, Pinterest, YouTube, TikTok, GMB, Threads, Mastodon, and Bluesky, all four tools report organic metrics only — either because there's no paid model or because the APIs don't expose paid data.

All four tools report paid impressions and reach for Facebook. The gap widens at the organic level: Buffer cannot report organic impressions for Facebook at all — Meta's API removed that data. Zoho Social is the only tool that also reports viral vs. non-viral views for Facebook posts. For LinkedIn, Zoho Social's organic vs. sponsored follower chart is included on standard plans; Sprout Social requires Premium Analytics and Hootsuite requires Advanced Analytics for the equivalent.

08 — Sentiment analysis & social listening

Understand what people are saying — and how they feel

Social listening and sentiment analysis answer a different question than post metrics: not "how did my content perform?" but "what is the broader conversation around my brand?" Buffer has no social listening or sentiment analysis at all. Sprout Social places most sentiment features behind its Advanced plan, with a separate paid Listening add-on required for real-time monitoring and AI classification. Hootsuite includes keyword and hashtag tracking on all plans via Listening Basics, but full AI-powered sentiment requires the enterprise Talkwalker tier.

Zoho Social includes real-time sentiment monitoring, brand health tracking, AI classification, and keyword-level sentiment scoring as standard — no add-on required. Its listening covers Facebook, X, Instagram, LinkedIn, GMB, YouTube, Bluesky, and Threads. Sprout's Listening add-on adds Reddit, Tumblr, and the web. Hootsuite's Talkwalker layer covers 13+ networks plus 30+ news and forum sources at enterprise level.

09 — Competitor analysis

See how you stack up — without asking them

Competitor analysis lets you benchmark your social performance against rivals, track how often they post, and monitor the conversations happening around their brands. This is where the add-on model of competitors becomes most apparent. Buffer has no competitor analysis capabilities at all. Sprout Social's Competitor Reports — covering Facebook, Instagram, and X — are available on Professional and Advanced plans, but keyword and mention tracking for competitors requires the separate Listening add-on. Hootsuite supports competitive benchmarking on Standard plans (up to 5 competitors) and Advanced plans (up to 20), including posting frequency, audience growth, and industry benchmarking through Talkwalker.

Zoho Social includes competitor profile monitoring, keyword and hashtag tracking for competitors, and mention tracking without requiring a paid add-on. You can monitor competitor pages without following them. Formal performance benchmarking (engagement vs. competitor, audience growth comparison) is less explicitly documented than in Sprout or Hootsuite, so teams that need structured benchmark reports may find Hootsuite or Sprout more complete for that specific use case.

14 — CRM & helpdesk integration

From social interaction to sales pipeline

Most social media tools stop at reporting. Zoho Social goes a step further by connecting analytics to action — turning social interactions directly into business outcomes. Its native integration with Zoho CRM (available on Premium, Agency, and Agency Plus plans) lets you create leads automatically from likes, mentions, comments, messages, and new followers, enrich existing CRM contact records with social profile data, and convert social posts into Zoho Desk support tickets — all within the same platform ecosystem, with no third-party connector required.

Sprout Social's CRM connectivity is broader in terms of which external platforms it supports — Salesforce (preferred partner), HubSpot, Zendesk, and Microsoft Dynamics — making it a stronger option for teams already on Salesforce. Hootsuite connects to Salesforce and HubSpot. Buffer has no CRM integration of any kind. If your team is already deep in the Zoho ecosystem, Zoho Social's native integration eliminates the connector setup and data sync overhead entirely. If you're on Salesforce, Sprout Social is likely the better fit for CRM-linked social workflows.
