11 — Additional capabilities

UTM tracking, historical data, and CRM integration

UTM tracking

All four tools support UTM parameters and Google Analytics integration, so you can trace social traffic through to conversion. The meaningful difference is in plan gating. Zoho Social makes UTM available on paid plans (Standard and above), which is comparable to Sprout Social (Professional and Advanced) and Hootsuite (Professional+). Buffer includes UTM on all paid plans but limits the networks that receive tags — only Facebook, X, LinkedIn, YouTube Shorts, GMB, Bluesky, TikTok, and Threads. Zoho Social applies UTM to all connected platforms without network exclusions.

Custom UTM parameters are supported by all four tools, but Buffer and Sprout Social restrict custom parameter editing to paid plans only.

Historical data on connect

When you first connect a channel, how far back does each tool reach? This varies significantly. Buffer has the strongest historical import at connect — up to 6 months of channel-level Facebook data, 30 days of Instagram posts, 100 X posts (up to 30 days), 6 months of LinkedIn channel data, and 250 Pinterest pins. Hootsuite also backfills data, including Facebook post-level history. Zoho Social imports channel-level Facebook history on connect but does not backfill post-level data for Instagram, X, LinkedIn, or YouTube. Sprout Social starts from the connection date for most networks, though Instagram supports up to 2 years of historical metrics. Hootsuite's data goes back to January 2018 for supported networks.

For year-over-year comparison, Hootsuite and Sprout Social have a clear advantage — both allow custom date ranges that span historical periods. Zoho Social and Buffer can do year-over-year only after 1+ year of continuous use on the platform, since they don't backfill data from before the connection date.

CRM and helpdesk integration

Zoho Social is the only tool here with a fully native CRM connection — it integrates directly with Zoho CRM (Premium, Agency, and Agency Plus plans) to create leads from social mentions, comments, follows, and messages, and converts interactions to Zoho Desk support tickets. The social contact card is visible inside Zoho CRM, and UTM data flows through to track lead source.

Sprout Social connects to Salesforce (preferred partner), HubSpot, Zendesk, and Microsoft Dynamics, allowing social data to appear inside CRM records and routing conversations to support teams. Hootsuite offers Salesforce, HubSpot, and Zendesk integrations with similar capabilities. Buffer has no native CRM integration — it focuses entirely on publishing and analytics without a customer engagement layer.

If your team is already in the Zoho ecosystem, Zoho Social's native integration removes any connector overhead. If you're on Salesforce or HubSpot, Sprout and Hootsuite both provide deeper existing integrations.

Best time to post

Zoho Social's SmartQ recommends audience-driven optimal posting times across all connected networks. It's available on Standard plans and above and doesn't require a minimum follower or post threshold. Hootsuite offers best time to post across all major networks (Instagram, Facebook, X, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, Pinterest) using the last 30 days of engagement data, with research-based fallbacks when account history is thin. Sprout Social's Optimal Send Times works across supported networks but requires sufficient historical engagement data to generate recommendations, and is limited to Professional and Advanced plans. Buffer's equivalent is Instagram-only and requires at least 100 followers and 10 posts within the past 7 days — so it's effectively unavailable for newer or smaller accounts.
