01 — Platform coverage

Analytics across the widest network range

Zoho Social provides analytics for 11 platforms — more than any tool in this comparison. The biggest gaps in competing tools: Google Business Profile is unsupported by Hootsuite, Sprout Social, and Buffer; Mastodon analytics are exclusive to Zoho Social. Bluesky is available in Zoho Social and Sprout Social, but not in Hootsuite or Buffer. On the core four — X, Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn — all tools are on equal footing. All four tools explicitly do not support WhatsApp, Telegram, Instagram Personal, Facebook Groups, or LinkedIn Personal for analytics.

Buffer's analytics scope is the most limited: TikTok, YouTube, Threads, Pinterest, Mastodon, and Bluesky are all absent. Its full Analyze dashboard is also restricted to paid plans — free users get a basic "Sent" tab only.

For X threads, all tools except Buffer report full thread metrics — Buffer tracks the first post only. For LinkedIn, Zoho Social and Sprout Social both support analytics for personal profile posts; Hootsuite and Buffer do not.

03 — Data freshness & historical data

Real-time updates and historical depth

Zoho Social updates analytics in real time. Sprout Social applies platform-dependent delays (Facebook demographics lag 48–72 hours). Hootsuite refreshes every few minutes but Facebook reports only update once daily. Buffer applies up to a 24-hour delay for most networks (6 hours for boosted posts). LinkedIn data refreshes after 24 hours across all four tools — a LinkedIn API constraint, not a platform-level choice.

On historical depth, Hootsuite offers the most breadth — data backfilled to January 1, 2018 across networks, with Instagram audience and Stories backfill on connect. Buffer provides meaningful backfill on connect: 6 months for Facebook and LinkedIn channel-level, 30 days for Instagram posts. Zoho Social and Sprout Social largely start from the connection date, with Sprout providing up to 2 years of Instagram data as an exception.

04 — Audience demographics

Know exactly who you're reaching

Zoho Social covers audience demographics across five platforms — Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, and Pinterest. Its LinkedIn breakdown is the deepest available in this comparison: industry, job function, seniority, company size, experience level, and location. Hootsuite covers the widest set of platforms for demographics, adding TikTok and X to the mix. Sprout Social covers Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn. Buffer is most limited — full demographics only for Instagram, and location only for Facebook.

Important on Facebook demographics: Meta deprecated age and gender audience data across its API on November 15, 2025. All four tools now show follower count and location only for Facebook — this is a Meta API change, not a platform choice. Claims in page copy should focus on LinkedIn, Instagram, and YouTube demographics.

05 — Organic vs paid analytics

Separate what you earned from what you spent

All four tools report paid impressions and reach for Facebook and LinkedIn. The split becomes more nuanced at the organic level. For Facebook, Buffer cannot report organic impressions at all — Meta's API removed that data. Zoho Social is unique in reporting viral vs. non-viral views for Facebook. For LinkedIn, Zoho Social provides a dedicated organic-vs-sponsored follower chart from standard plans; Sprout Social requires Premium Analytics, and Hootsuite requires Advanced Analytics.

For X, Pinterest, YouTube, TikTok, GMB, Threads, Mastodon, and Bluesky: all four tools report organic metrics only. These networks either have no paid model or their APIs do not expose paid performance data.

06 — Sentiment analysis & social listening

Built-in intelligence — not a bolt-on

Buffer offers no sentiment analysis or social listening capabilities. Sprout Social places most sentiment features behind its Advanced plan and an additional paid Listening add-on. Hootsuite includes Listening Basics on all plans (keyword columns, hashtag tracking, mention monitoring) but reserves AI-powered Talkwalker intelligence for enterprise tiers. Zoho Social includes real-time sentiment monitoring, brand health tracking, and keyword-level sentiment scoring as standard — no add-on required.

On social listening coverage, Zoho Social monitors FB, X, IG, LinkedIn, GMB, YouTube, Bluesky, and Threads. Sprout's Listening add-on extends to Reddit, Tumblr, and the web. Hootsuite's Talkwalker layer covers 13+ networks plus 30+ news and forum sources at the enterprise tier. Notably, Zoho Social is also the only tool here that monitors Google Business Reviews and Questions natively — Hootsuite added GMB review visibility to its Inbox in April 2026, but only as a monitoring/engagement feature, not full analytics.

UTM tagging is available across all four tools on paid plans. Zoho's UTM support spans all connected platforms; Buffer applies it to a defined subset only — Facebook, X, LinkedIn, YouTube Shorts, GMB, Bluesky, TikTok, and Threads.

08 — Reports, exports & cross-channel view

Reports built for sharing — not just dashboards

All four tools export in PDF, CSV, and image format. The differences are in automation, customization, and cross-channel scope. Zoho Social includes scheduled report delivery (daily/weekly/monthly) on Professional and Premium plans, agency-branded white-label reports, and a drag-and-drop custom report builder. Buffer has none of these — no scheduled delivery, no white-labeling on entry-tier paid plans, and no automated cross-channel reporting. Sprout Social's custom report builder (My Reports) requires a Premium Analytics add-on; Hootsuite's full builder requires the Team plan.

Custom date ranges in Zoho Social depend on plan tier: 15 days for Free/Standard, 3 months for Professional, and 1 year for Premium and Agency plans. Sprout and Hootsuite offer flexible date ranges on paid plans. Buffer restricts free users to preset ranges only (Last 7/30 days, Month to Date).

09 — Analytics on the free plan

Real analytics before you pay a cent

Sprout Social and Hootsuite have no permanent free plan — only 30-day trials, after which analytics access disappears unless you subscribe. Both Zoho Social and Buffer offer permanent free plans, but their scope differs significantly. Zoho Social's free plan includes actual post metrics for published posts. Buffer's free equivalent is limited to a "Sent" tab showing likes, comments, and impressions for posts scheduled through Buffer — it's not a full analytics view.

Audience demographics and custom reports are unavailable on the free plan of any tool in this comparison. For teams that want to fully evaluate analytics before committing, trial lengths are: Zoho Social (15 days), Buffer (14 days), Sprout Social (30 days), Hootsuite (30 days).

10 — CRM & helpdesk integration

From social interaction to sales pipeline

Zoho Social's CRM integration is native — connecting directly to Zoho CRM (Premium, Agency, and Agency Plus plans) and Zoho Desk. You can create leads manually or automatically from social interactions including likes, mentions, comments, messages, and new followers; enrich CRM contacts with social profile data; and convert social posts directly into support tickets — all within the same ecosystem.

Sprout Social integrates with Salesforce (preferred partner), HubSpot, Zendesk, and Microsoft Dynamics. Hootsuite connects with Salesforce and HubSpot. Both require a third-party CRM subscription and integration setup. Buffer has no CRM integration.
